Design work

Welcome to the page of some of my design work!

These are a few of my best works, please keep in mind that most of these are assignments and if I had more time, I feel like I would have done a better job on some of these. Either way, please don't take/use any of my work without giving credit, also keep in mind that some of these images may use copyrighted content, as they were originally assignments and not intended to be published!

Also, keep in mind that the spacing in between these images/text area are equal! The images are just different sizes, making it look like there are different size margins. Please ignore this!

This project was really fun! This was a Photoshop project where we had to take an image and blend it into a background. I feel like I did a pretty good job on it and I'm happy with how this one turned out.

This was our first assignment in the class. We utilized Adobe Illustrator to create images that look like different flags. This was before we learned how to measure elements on images and I created these using the shape-related tools, and I used the pen tool for the Canada flag. I am pretty happy with these turned out as they were pretty similar to the originals. I also did not use the eye-dropper tool to pick the colors as I had to eyeball them. 

Here is my recreation of the Google Chrome logo. When I first started making this one, I really felt that it would look horrible in the end. This is when we were first learning how to use the Pen Tool in Adobe Illustrator. I started trying to trace over the original logo but it just looked really bad. I started getting creative and I started using the Shape Tool to make the outlines, and then used the Pen Tool to trace over the inner-edges and I ended up with this after a bit of work. I am really proud of how this turned out and the struggle was worth it. 

Utilizing the Clone Stamp Tool, I managed to remove the watermark on the bottom right corner of this image. I am really happy of how this one turned out and it is a really handy skill to have. I chose this image of water as I thought it would be fun to try and remove the watermark from a more complex image that has many different shades of a color.

This assignment required us to take an image of a box and use coloring options to blend a logo onto it. We used different coloring filters in Adobe Illustrator to make the Starbucks logo blend onto the blank sides of the box. When we first started this one, I was a bit confused. However, I feel like it turned out fine in the end. I feel like it could've gone better. However, it's better than what I could've done before I learned the skills for this assignment.

This assignment was one of my most favorite ones. The image you see above is the result of two different assignments. We used the Pen Tool to draw out different triangle-style shapes on a second layer above an empty circle. After all of the triangles had been drawn out, we used the clipping mask to mask over the overlapping ones to make a perfect circle over the triangles. We made a gradient on the circle and then carried over the colors onto the triangles above the circle. After all of this, we used the effect gallery to appl an effect to the image. The second assignment was to create an icon for the class's Discord server. What I did is apply another effect to the image then use the ASUS ROG font to add the text on top of the image. After all of this, I applied a final effect and that is what you see above. I had a lot of fun with this one and it is one of my best works.
